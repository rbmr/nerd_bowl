### FEB21020X-25: Weekly Overview

**Chapter 1. Introduction.**
* Topics: Introduction, Microeconomics (Micro), Rational Choice Theory, No Profitable Deviation Property, Reasoning at The Margin, Chapter Summary.
* Concepts: the rational choice theory, the utility maximization paradigm, and the utility maximization problem (UMP); substantive rationality; monotone transformation of utility, status quo and the opportunity costs; the cost-benefit approach, assessing costs and benefits using reservation prices; willingness to pay and willingness to accept; the First-Order Condition (FOC).
* Topics for Exercises: Compute optimal choice in simple binary choice examples, accounting for opportunity costs and neglecting sunk costs.

**Chapter 2. Supply and Demand.**
* Topics: Introduction, Individual Demand and Market Demand, Individual Supply and Market Supply, Equilibrium, Welfare Economics, Chapter Summary.
* Concepts: Markets, single-price markets, competitive markets, price-taking behavior; inverse demand; the law of demand; determinants of demand; inverse supply; the law of supply; determinants of Supply; excess demand and supply, market equilibrium (EQ); EQ existence, uniqueness, multiplicity, and stability; welfare economics, consumer surplus CS, producer surplus $\pi$, social welfare SW; positive and normative economics; price ceilings and caps, the deadweight loss (DWL); Pareto efficiency.
* Topics for Exercises: Compute market demand (supply) from individual demands (supplies), compute market EQ from demand and supply, check the EQ stability, compute the outcome of market regulations: setting price caps or floors, compute consumer surplus, producer surplus, social welfare, and the deadweight loss from market price regulations (price caps and floors).

**Chapter 3. Game Theory.**
* Topics: Introduction, Language of GT, Equilibria Based on Dominance, Nash Equilibrium, Sequential Games.
* Concepts: Player, strategy, payoff function; strategy profile; the normal form of a game; the bi-matrix representation of the normal form; solving the game; EQ concept and EQ; EQ and the importance of information; dominant strategy; outcomes and Pareto efficiency; elimination, iterative dominance, reduced-form game; strictly dominated strategy (SDS), iterative elimination of SDSs (IESDS), common knowledge; Nash EQ, best response; coordination problem, NE existence problem; random strategies and existence; simultaneous-move, static, sequential-move, dynamic games.

**Chapter 3 cont. Game Theory. Slides 41-55.**
* Topics: Game Tree, Subgame Perfect Nash Equilibrium (SPNE), Chapter Summary.
* Concepts: The extensive form of a game: nodes, moves, information sets; subgames and subgame perfection, subgame perfect Nash equilibrium (SPNE), backward induction (BI), and the reduced-form game.
* Games studied in the class: The game from page 47 (guessing $2/3$ of the average guess), the "Prisoners Dilemma" game, the "Cheryl's birthday" problem on common knowledge, the "Meeting in New-York" game of Thomas Schelling, the Nash demand game (Nash bargaining game), The "Matching Pennies" game, the "Battle of the Sexes" game (BoS), static and dynamic.
* Excluded: incomplete information and behavioral game theory (pages 65-69).
* Topics for Exercises: Compute dominant strategies, IDS, SDS, dominant EQ, best responses, NE, Compute SPNE for games with perfect information.

**Chapter 4. Consumer Choice. Slides 1-25.**
* Topics: Introduction, Walrasian Budget Set, Consumer Preferences and the Utility Function.
* Concepts: Consumption bundle, inner product, wealth, exogenous and endogenous variables; budget constraint, opportunity set, Walrasian budget set; preference relation, completeness and transitivity; contour sets; indifference curve; ordinal and cardinal properties, monotone transformations; continuous and monotone preferences.

**Chapter 4 cont. Consumer Choice.**
* Topics: The Marginal Rate of Substitution - MRS, The UMP - Utility Maximization Problem, Using LM to find Corner Solutions, Common Types of Utility Functions / Preferences, Chapter Summary.
* Concepts: Gossen's first law, convex preferences; Walrasian demand, Walras law, indirect utility function, interior and corner solutions, Lagrange method (LM), Gossen's second law; Cobb-Douglas, linear, quasi-linear, and Leontief preferences/utility.
* Excluded: non-linear budget constraints (81-82), more-is-worse and satiation points (91), an application of the rational choice model (97-99), theory of choice and household production (99-100).
* Topics for Exercises: Solving the UMP for interior and corner solutions (Walrasian demand and indirect utility).

**Chapter 5. Individual and Market Demands.**
* Topics: Introduction, The Effect of Changes in Income, Elasticity, The Effect of Changes in Prices, Hicksian Decomposition, Again the Elasticity, When is the Wealth Effect Zero (Example A.5.5), Market Demand, Chapter Summary.
* Concepts: Comparative statics; the income-consumption curve, normal, inferior, luxury, and necessity goods; Engel curve, demand elasticity, the price-consumption curve, Hicksian wealth compensation, Hicksian demand, the substitution and the wealth effects, willingness to accept (WTA), willingness to pay (WTP), Giffen good; substitution elasticity, quasi-linear utility, the numeraire.
* Excluded: Veblen good (130), the importance of income distribution (148-149), forecasting economic trends (149-150).
* Topics for Exercises: Computing Hicksian compensation, demand, and decomposition, computing elasticities.

**Chapter 6. Applications for Welfare Economics.**
* Topics: Introduction, Market Equilibrium and Tax Incidence, Consumer Surplus, Chapter Summary.
* Concepts: Marshallian consumer surplus (CS), social welfare (SW), commodity taxation, Compensating Variation (CV) and Equivalent Variation (EV).
* Excluded: a bias in the consumer price index (176-177), the income-compensated demand curve (178-181), compensating variation (182-184, we do it in class), tax burden and equivalent variation (186-188, we do it in class).
* Topics for Exercises: Computing market equilibria with a commodity tax, computing CS and DWL, computing CV and EV.

**Chapter 7. Choice under Uncertainty.**
* Topics: Introduction, Money Lotteries, Bernoulli Utility Function, Von Neumann-Morgenstern Expected Utility Function (EU function).
* Concepts: Lottery, the cumulative distribution function (CDF); discrete, continuous, and singular random variables (RVs); the support of a RV, the probability density function (PDF), the expected value of a RV, The St. Petersburg paradox, the Bernoulli Utility function, the Dual Theory of Choice under Uncertainty, von Neumann-Morgenstern Expected Utility (EU) function, the Expected Utility theorem, the Independence Axiom (IA).

**Chapter 7 cont. Choice under Uncertainty. **
* Topics: Certainty Equivalent (CE) Risk Attitudes, Insurance, Informational Economics, Chapter Summary.
* Concepts: The Certainty Equivalent, willingness to pay (WTP) for a lottery, comparing lotteries; risk aversion, neutrality, and seeking; accounting for initial wealth, preference reversal, the optimal stopping rule, the acceptance wage, a threshold strategy,
* From the textbook: EU and the rational choice model, the economics of information, adverse selection, moral hazard, signaling.
* Topics for Exercises: Computing expected values, expected utilities, and certainty equivalents for lotteries, computing the $WTP=CV$ for a lottery.

**Chapter 10. Production.**
* Topics: Production function, Short Run (SR) and Long Run (LR), Law of Diminishing Returns, Total Product Curve, Marginal Product, Average Product, Relationship between Curves, MRTS, Returns to Scale, Isoquant Maps.
* Appendix: Cobb-Douglas and Leontief production functions, and mathematical definition of returns to scale (p. 307 to 313).

**Chapter 11. Costs.**
* Topics: Costs in the short run, the Relationship between MP, AP, MC, and AVC, Cost minimization and allocating production between two processes, Costs in the long run,
* Appendix: Deriving the cost function.

**Chapter 12. Perfect competition.**
* Topics: Goal of profit maximization, Four conditions for perfect competition, Short run condition for profit maximization, Shutdown condition, Short-run competitive industry supply, Short run competitive equilibrium, Efficiency of short-run competitive equilibrium, Adjustments in the long run, the Invisible hand.

**Chapter 13. Monopoly.**
* Topics: Defining monopoly, Five sources of monopoly, the Profit-maximizing monopolist, Total revenue curve, Marginal revenue, Optimality condition, Marginal revenue and elasticity, Graphing marginal revenue, Mark-up, Shutdown condition, Monopolist has no supply curve, Efficiency loss from monopoly, Public policy towards natural monopoly, Price discrimination (1st, 2nd and 3rd degree).

**Chapter 14. Imperfect competition.**
* Topics: Hotelling model, Cournot model, Bertrand model, Stackelberg model, Comparison of outcomes, Entry deterrence, Collusion.

**Chapter 17. General equilibrium.**
* Topics: General equilibrium analysis, Simple exchange economy, Edgeworth exchange box, Efficiency and fairness, Market exchange, How prices adjust, Invisible hand theorem, Efficiency in production, Efficiency in product mix.

**Chapter 18. Externalities and property rights.**
* Topics: Externalities and market efficiency, Externalities in markets, Reciprocal nature of externalities, Coase theorem, Positive externalities, Property rights, Tragedy of the Commons, Positional externalities, Taxing externalities.
